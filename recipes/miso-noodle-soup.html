Miso Noodle Soup

Description

This Japanese styled miso noodle soup is a perfect choise on a cold winter day. The soup gets its taste from miso paste and peanut butter.

Ingredients

  • 50 grams of spring onion
  • 2 tablespoons of grated ginger in sesame oil
  • 3 tablespoons of light miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons of smooth peanut butter
  • 12 dl of water
  • 250 grams of tofu
  • 1 tablespoons of canola oil
  • 250 grams of bok choy
  • 90 grams of Udon noodles
  • 2-4 tablespoons of crunchy garlic in chili sesame oil

Steps

  1. Finely chop the spring onion. Place the grated ginger and white parts of the spring onion into a saucepan. Sauté for a few minutes over low heat. Reserve the green parts of the spring onion fir garnish.
  2. Add the miso paste, peanut butter and water to the saucepan. Simmer gently under a lid for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Press the tofu using paper towels or a clean kitchen towel, then cut it into cubes. If desired, fry the tofu in oil until golden brown.
  4. Rinse the bok choy. Separate the leaves and, if necessary, cut the largest leaves into smaller pieces.
  5. Add the tofu, bok choy and noodles to the broth. Cook for 4-5 minutes until the noodles are tender and the bok choy has softened slightly. Finally add the remaining spring onion. Serve the soup with crispy garlic in chili sesame oil.
